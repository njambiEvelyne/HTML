Food choices that support recovery

Simple, evidence-informed food recommendations to support people during illness. Includes gentle recipes, benefits and practical precautions. Always consult your healthcare provider for personalised advice.

See Recommendations Get a Plan

Recommended Foods by Symptom

Choose foods tailored to common needs: gentle on the stomach, immune-supporting, energy-restoring, and hydrating options.

Gentle & Easy-to-Digest

  • Plain porridge or congee
  • Steamed vegetables (carrot, zucchini)
  • Boiled potatoes or mashed sweet potato
  • Bananas and applesauce

When to use: nausea, stomach flu, post-surgery recovery.

Immune Support

  • Chicken or vegetable broth
  • Citrus fruits, kiwi (vitamin C)
  • Yogurt with live cultures
  • Garlic and ginger (in moderation)

When to use: colds, respiratory infections (complementary to treatment)

Energy & Protein

  • Lean proteins: eggs, fish, poultry
  • Legumes and soft tofu
  • Whole grains (oats, brown rice)

When to use: prolonged illness or recovery to prevent muscle loss.

Hydration & Electrolytes

  • Oral rehydration solutions or broths
  • Herbal teas, diluted fruit juice
  • Water-rich fruits: watermelon, oranges

When to use: fever, vomiting, diarrhea, heat-related illness.

Benefits of Recommended Foods

  • Supports immune function: nutrient-dense foods supply vitamins and minerals needed for recovery.
  • Reduces digestive stress: bland, low-fiber choices soothe an upset stomach.
  • Maintains strength: protein-rich options preserve muscle and support healing.
  • Restores hydration: fluids and electrolytes prevent dehydration and help medications work properly.

Precautions & When to Avoid

Certain foods can interfere with medications or exacerbate conditions. Use caution and ask your clinician when in doubt.

  • Spicy or greasy foods: may worsen nausea or reflux.
  • High-fiber foods: can be hard to tolerate during acute diarrhea unless reintroduced slowly.
  • Raw or undercooked foods: avoid if immune-compromised.
  • Interactions: grapefruit and some medications interact—check with your pharmacist.
